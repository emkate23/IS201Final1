So You're Bored on a Friday Night...

Maybe your date cancelled, your friends are out of town, or you just had a long week at work and decided that what you need right now is to feel something. Like really feel something.

The obvious choice is to throw on a movie. More specifically, a romantic comedy (or romcom for short.) If you can't tell, I am speaking from experience.

Romcoms are a genius invention. They combined some of the most profitable movie genres on the market, romance, drama, and comedy, to create a whole new one.


Categories of Romcoms

Before we get into the overall ranking, we need to chat about the various categories of these guilty pleasure movies. Theses include (but are not limited to):

  • Nicholas Sparks Movies
  • Adam Sandler Romcoms

    • My unofficial top 3:

    1. The Wedding Singer
    2. 50 First Dates
    3. Just Go with it

  • 2 Hours of Swooning Over the Love Interest
  • Comroms (Mostly Com, not much Rom)

Not all of these categories are created equal, but I am going to combine them all for an overall ranking.

#5: 10 Things I Hate About You

From Heath Ledger seranading Julia Stiles to the epic paint fight kiss, I love all aspects of this movie. It follows the classic enemies to lovers pipeline, what more could you ask for?


#4: When Harry Met Sally

This film is a classic. The friendship. The laughs. The sweaters. It's perfect for a fall evening.


#3: Clueless

This is one of my personal favorites. It is funny, Cher is an icon, it's practically a perfect movie. The reason it didn't land higher is because I think it's weird to date your ex-step brother.


#2: How to Lose a Guy in 10 Days

You didn't think I'd forget this, did you? Matthew McConaughey is a wonderful lead, and Kate Hudson perfectly embodies her character. This is a lot of people's #1.


#1: Breakfast at Tiffany's

This movie is the ultimate romcom. It is funny, has strange but complete characters, and a perfect twist. This will forever be the winner to me. The last kiss in the rain? I mean, come on!
